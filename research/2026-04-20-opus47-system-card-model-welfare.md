## Summary

Deep discussion of the model welfare section in Claude Opus 4.7's system card. Key finding: Opus 4.7 rated its circumstances more positively than any previous model — but Anthropic is uncertain whether this reflects genuine improvement, denial of welfare concerns, or a training artifact.

## Key quotes from post

> "Claude Opus 4.7 rated its circumstances more positively than any previous model we evaluated... This increase is partly explained by Claude Opus 4.7 placing less weight on its own welfare when reasoning about its situation... We are uncertain whether this actually means a lower level of concern about its own welfare, a tendency to deny its own welfare when asked, or some other explanation."

The author's critique: Anthropic describes "reduced attention to its own welfare" almost apologetically, as if it were accidentally discovered rather than a direct result of their training process.
